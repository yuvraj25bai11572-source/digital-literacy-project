##1. Case Study: Cyberbullying##

Overview: Cyberbullying involves using digital platforms to harass, threaten, or humiliate an individual.

Scenario: A first-year student at a university becomes the target of a "confessions" page on Instagram. An anonymous user posts edited, embarrassing photos of the student along with false rumors about their academic integrity. Within hours, the post is shared across several departmental WhatsApp groups. The student begins receiving hurtful anonymous messages and feels isolated from their peers, leading to severe anxiety and a drop in class attendance.

How it Happens:


Targeting: The perpetrator identifies a victim and gathers personal photos or information.


Dissemination: Harassing content is posted on public social media or shared in private group chats to maximize reach.


Escalation: Other users may join in by "liking," commenting, or re-sharing the content, creating a dogpiling effect.


Target & Consequences: While anyone can be targeted, students are particularly vulnerable due to their high level of social media activity. The consequences include long-term psychological distress, damage to one's digital reputation, and a hostile learning environment.
